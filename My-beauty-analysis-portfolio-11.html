Soft Summer Sneaker Test

Use these 3 quick tests in-store or when your order arrives. If it fails 2+ tests, it's probably too warm for your palette.

Paper Test

Hold the sneaker next to a sheet of white printer paper. Soft Summer greys should look cool/neutral against white, not creamy, beige, or yellow.

Face Test

Hold the shoe near your face in natural light. Does it make your skin look grey/ashy or rosy/bright? Soft Summer harmonies bring out rose in cheeks, not sallow tones.

Silver Test

Compare the shoe's midsole/accents to silver jewellery. Cool greys should harmonize with silver. If it looks better with gold/tan, it's too warm for you.

TOSS: Avoid These

These will fight your palette

Soft Summer needs cool, muted tones. These warm/yellow-based options will make outfits look off.

❌

Earth Strata Campus

The brown/green earth tones are too warm and saturated for Soft Summer

❌

Warm Browns & Tans

Caramel, tan, coffee - anything with yellow/orange base. Stick to cool taupe/greige instead

❌

Yellow or Gum Soles

Yellow midsoles instantly warm up the whole shoe. Look for white, grey, or off-white soles only
